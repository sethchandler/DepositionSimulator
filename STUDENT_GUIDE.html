üöÄ Getting Started: Your First Deposition

1

Choose Your AI Assistant

First, select which AI system will power your simulation. You have several options:

  • OpenAI (ChatGPT): Most reliable and sophisticated responses
  • Google Gemini: Good alternative with strong reasoning
  • Ollama (Local): Runs on your computer (technical setup required)

For beginners, we recommend OpenAI. You'll need to enter your API key (think of it as your password for the AI service).

2

Pick a Case Scenario

Choose from several realistic legal scenarios, each with a different type of witness:

  • John Sterling (Homicide): Eyewitness hiding an affair
  • Susan Miller (Employment): HR manager concealing age discrimination
  • Jeffrey Hinton (Sexual Harassment): Vice President denying inappropriate conduct
  • Dr. McGee (Medical Malpractice): Surgeon defending failed procedure

Each scenario includes realistic documents that can help you catch the witness in lies.

3

Start Questioning

Simply type your questions in the chat box and press Enter or click Send. The AI witness will respond as a real person would, complete with evasions, emotions, and attempts to hide damaging information.

üéØ Effective Questioning Techniques

Start with Safe Territory

Begin with background questions that establish the witness's credentials and basic facts. This builds your foundation and helps the witness become comfortable (perhaps too comfortable).

Good opening questions:
"Please state your name for the record."
"What is your current occupation?"
"How long have you worked at TechCorp?"
"Can you describe your typical duties?"

Build Your Timeline

Establish a clear sequence of events. Get the witness to commit to specific dates, times, and locations. This creates a framework you can later challenge with documents.

Timeline building:
"Where were you on the evening of March 23rd?"
"What time did you leave the office?"
"Did you go directly home?"
"Did you stop anywhere along the way?"

Use the Funnel Technique

Start broad, then narrow down to specific details. This prevents witnesses from wiggling out later by claiming they misunderstood the question.

Funnel sequence:
"Did you have any communications that evening?" (broad)
"Did you make any phone calls?" (narrower)
"Did you call anyone between 11 PM and midnight?" (specific)
"Did you call Maria Martinez at 11:47 PM?" (pinpoint)

Lock in Their Story

Before impeaching with documents, make sure the witness is committed to their version of events. Use phrases like "Are you certain?" and "Is there anything you'd like to add?"

üìÑ Working with Documents

Review Documents First

Before questioning begins, read all available documents. Look for:

  • Dates and times that contradict witness statements
  • Communications the witness might deny
  • Financial records showing motives
  • Evidence of knowledge the witness claims not to have

The Art of Impeachment

When you find a contradiction between the witness's testimony and a document, follow this sequence:

  1. Confront without the document first: "Are you certain you didn't call anyone that night?"
  2. Give them a chance to correct: "Would it refresh your memory if I showed you phone records?"
  3. Show the document: "I'm showing you Exhibit A, your phone records for March 23rd..."
  4. Make the contradiction clear: "This shows a call to Maria Martinez at 11:47 PM, doesn't it?"
Effective impeachment sequence:
You: "You testified that you went straight home after work, correct?"
Witness: "Yes, that's right."
You: "You didn't stop anywhere?"
Witness: "No."
You: "I'm showing you what's been marked as Exhibit B, a hotel receipt from the Starlight Motel dated March 23rd. Do you see your name on this receipt?"
Witness: "I... well, I may have stopped briefly to rest..."

‚öñÔ∏è Handling Objections and Difficult Situations

When the Judge Gets Involved

If you've enabled Judge Mode, opposing counsel may object to your questions. Here's how to handle common objections:

  • "Objection, leading!": Rephrase as an open-ended question
  • "Objection, relevance!": Explain how this relates to the case
  • "Objection, asked and answered!": Move on or explain why you need clarification
Handling a relevance objection:
Opposing Counsel: "Objection, Your Honor. The witness's personal relationships aren't relevant to this contract dispute."
You: "Your Honor, the witness's credibility is at issue. Evidence that he's lied about his personal relationships bears on whether this court should believe his testimony about the business relationship."

When Witnesses Become Evasive

AI witnesses are programmed to be realistic, which means they'll try to avoid damaging admissions. Here are techniques for dealing with evasive witnesses:

  • Pin them down: "Please answer yes or no"
  • Repeat the question: "You haven't answered my question. Did you or didn't you?"
  • Use documents: Show them evidence that makes denial impossible
  • Ask the judge for help: "Your Honor, could you please instruct the witness to answer the question?"

When the Judge Seems Unfair

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Is This Realistic or a Problem?

If the judge seems biased or makes questionable rulings, ask yourself: "Could this happen in real life?" Often, the answer is yes. Difficult judges are part of the legal landscape, and learning to handle them is valuable training.

When it's realistic training: The judge is gruff but makes legally sound rulings

When it might be a problem: The judge makes clearly illegal rulings or behaves in ways no real judge would

If you think the AI is malfunctioning, try refreshing the page and starting over, or switch to a different AI provider.

üîß Troubleshooting Common Problems

Technical Issues

Error: "Failed to connect to API"

Solution: Check your internet connection and verify your API key is entered correctly. Make sure you have credits remaining with your AI provider.

Error: "Document not found"

Solution: Refresh the page and reload your scenario. If the problem persists, try a different scenario.

The witness isn't responding or gives very short answers

Solution: Try asking more specific, open-ended questions. If the problem continues, switch to a different AI model or refresh the page.

Simulation Issues

The witness admits everything too easily: Try a different scenario with a higher "perjury risk" witness, or ask more challenging questions that threaten their secrets.

The witness won't answer basic questions: The AI might be confused. Try rephrasing your question more clearly or start with simpler background questions.

Opposing counsel objects to everything: This might be realistic aggressive defense tactics. Argue your position or adjust your questioning style.

Getting Better Results

  • Be specific: Instead of "Tell me about that day," ask "What time did you arrive at the office on March 23rd?"
  • Use legal language: The AI responds better to proper deposition terminology
  • Stay in character: Address the witness directly, not the AI system
  • Be patient: Building a strong deposition record takes time and careful questioning

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Limitations

This Is Training, Not Reality

The Deposition Simulator is a valuable training tool, but it cannot replace real-world experience. Here's what it can and cannot do:

What it does well:

  • Teaches questioning techniques and document use
  • Provides safe practice with realistic scenarios
  • Helps you understand witness psychology
  • Builds confidence for real depositions

What it cannot do:

  • Replace supervision by experienced attorneys
  • Teach jurisdiction-specific procedural rules
  • Provide legal advice for actual cases
  • Guarantee success in real depositions

Ethical Considerations

  • Never use this simulator to practice on real case facts
  • Don't share confidential information with the AI system
  • Remember that AI responses are generated, not legal advice
  • Always follow your jurisdiction's rules for deposition practice
